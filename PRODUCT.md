# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Koma is for people who keep and read manga, doujinshi, western comics, webtoons, and graphic novels as local files or through services they control. It must work for someone opening one CBZ from Finder as naturally as it works for a collector maintaining a library with tens of thousands of books.

The primary workflow is simple: open or paste, begin reading, and return to the exact place later. Power users can organize, repair, convert, inspect, annotate, and tune the reading experience without making those tools visible to everyone else.

## Product Purpose

Koma is a local-first manga and comic reader for CBZ, CBR, other comic archives, image folders, PDF, and fixed-layout EPUB. It combines a fast page-first reader with a non-invasive library, rich metadata and repair tools, and permission-gated remote imports.

Success means the first book opens with almost no ceremony, the interface disappears while reading, every advanced operation is discoverable when needed, and every claimed feature has repeatable evidence on its supported platforms.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, exact, and assured.

Koma should feel native without becoming anonymous. It is calm around artwork, decisive in its language, responsive to every input, and comfortable at both casual and expert levels.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI dashboard styling, including gradient text, glass cards, oversized metrics, decorative blobs, and identical card grids.
- Anime-themed chrome, novelty Japanese motifs, fake paper textures, or ornamental decoration that competes with the book.
- Dense cockpit interfaces that expose every option at once.
- Generic Electron application styling, inconsistent controls, and web-page conventions that ignore the host platform.
- Copied layouts, assets, or source code from CBZen, Koharu, Panels, or any other reader.
- Motion that performs for the user instead of communicating state.

## Design Principles

1. **The page wins.** Reading surfaces give artwork the maximum space and controls recede until requested.
2. **Simple first, complete underneath.** Common actions are immediate. Expert tools use progressive disclosure, command search, and contextual inspectors.
3. **Native where familiarity matters.** Window behavior, menus, shortcuts, selection, focus, fields, and dialogs follow each platform's expectations.
4. **Files remain the user's.** The library is non-invasive, source edits are explicit and recoverable, and generated files are ordinary portable files.
5. **Evidence over claims.** Every state, format, and platform promise must be backed by tests, fixtures, benchmarks, or a clearly stated limitation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA for the application UI. Every workflow must support keyboard-only use, visible focus, screen readers, 200% interface zoom, reduced motion, high contrast, and color-independent state communication. Reader controls must remain operable with mouse, trackpad, touch, and keyboard. Automatic direction and layout choices must remain visible and manually overridable.
